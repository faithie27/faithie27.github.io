what remains of us

This poem was inspired by the idea of dancing being a metaphor for the crossing of personal boundaries.


            A shifting trembled throughout the earth
            And sand was lost in fault and crack
            My footprints defaced your self-worth
            Nothing I did could bring it back
                
            And even after the shaking’s quell
            My heel-print remained carved in stone
            The bond between us decayed and fell
            We still danced but now alone

fruit salad

            sifting and sorting through the red
            sweet and crunchy for too long a time
            so I decided to add a squeeze of lime

            but citric acid stung my tongue
            I glanced at all the different brands

House Fire

This poem is about someone with severe anxiety


              I thought my house was on fire
              So I pulled on some clothes and slid out of bed
              And ran downstairs to see only darkness instead
              I checked all the stoves, the pans and the steel
              But all I saw were the orange flames that had just seemed so real
              
              I thought my house was on fire
              So I dropped all my files and raced to my car
              Examined each wire and door left ajar
              I wondered which of my senses had played this cruel joke
              For I was still choking on each tendril of smoke

the frost

Internal rhymes and structural changes show how the reader comes to a realization about a loss

              
            You crept in like the frost that signals the beginning of a long winter
            I looked at you with the same wonder that I save for the stars
            At first you were so delicate – so beautiful – in shards
            But your love made up for the cuts on my hands
            I remember I took such care to not shatter you
            But then you grew – and so quickly too
            and consumed the leaves, the trees
            the cold ground bruised my knees
