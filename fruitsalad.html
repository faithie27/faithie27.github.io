fruit salad

                I found an apple at the grocery store
                the most perfect out of a hundred more
                I sliced it up into a bowl
                and kept it as food for my soul
                
                “Apples are just boring” – I said
                sifting and sorting through the red
                sweet and crunchy for too long a time
                so I decided to add a squeeze of lime
                
                It brought a freshness to my lungs
                but citric acid stung my tongue
                I glanced at all the different brands
                of rainbow I had at my hands
                
                I ate two mangoes, one old, one younger
                Hoping they would stay my hunger
                Gorged on grapes ‘til I nearly burst
                Hoping they would quench my thirst
                
                My head ached, my heart burned
                Mouth tingled, stomach churned
                Juice turned rancid, ice cooled
                The poison in my body pooled
                
                The bowl is empty, the apples brown
                in the dumpster a few blocks down
                and I have nothing else to say
                I regret tossing you away
